Credit Card Decline Handling SOP

Role: Founder / Account Manager
When: Payment fails in Stripe
Goal: Recover payment while maintaining relationship
Source: Kai Stone Systems Extra Useful Trainings
Last Updated: Feb 22, 2026

Why This Matters

Credit card declines are:

  1. An opportunity to check in on client satisfaction

  2. A chance to prevent churn before it happens

  3. Revenue that will be lost if not handled


Never let a decline sit. Handle within 24-48 hours.

The Process

Step 1: Check Stripe Weekly

  1. Go to Stripe Dashboard → Payments
  2. Filter by "Failed"
  3. Note all declined payments
  4. Add to your call list

Step 2: Call Them (Don't Just Text)

Why call:
  • Texts feel impersonal for billing issues
  • Call shows you care
  • Can gauge their satisfaction
  • Harder to ignore
Opening (Concerned, Not Accusatory):
"Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] from Slate Systems. How's everything going? I just wanted to check in — is everything okay on your end?"
If they ask why:
"I noticed there might have been an issue with your payment, and I wanted to make sure everything's good with your account. Did you recently get a new card or anything like that?"
Key: Lead with concern, not "you owe us money."

Step 3: Update Payment Method

If they have a new card:
  1. Go to Stripe → Customers → [Their Name]
  2. Click Payment Methods
  3. Click "+ Add" or update existing
  4. Enter new card details
  5. Manually charge if auto-retry exhausted
If they need to update themselves:
  • Send them the billing portal link
  • Walk them through it on the call if needed

Step 4: Use as Check-In Opportunity

While you have them:

  • "How's the website working out?"

  • "Getting any leads from it?"

  • "Anything I can help with?"


This turns a billing call into a retention call.

What If They Want to Cancel?

Don't just let them go. Get feedback:
"No problem at all. Before I process that, can I ask what's not working for you? I want to make sure we're doing right by our clients."
Common reasons and responses:
ReasonResponse
"Not getting leads""Let's look at your Google reviews and website together. Sometimes small tweaks help."
"Can't afford it""I understand. Want to pause for a month instead of canceling completely?"
"Not using it""What if I showed you how to use the review system? That's where most clients see value."
"Going a different direction""Totally respect that. Mind if I ask what you're switching to?"
If they still want to cancel: Process it gracefully. Don't burn the bridge.

Stripe Manual Charge Process

If auto-retry has exhausted (usually 4 attempts over 3-4 weeks):

  1. Stripe → Customers → [Customer]
  2. Verify correct payment method is on file
  3. Click "Create payment"
  4. Enter amount
  5. Select payment method
  6. Click "Charge"

Prevention: Reduce Future Declines

  1. Collect backup payment method during onboarding
  2. Send payment reminder 3 days before billing date
  3. Use Stripe's Smart Retries (enabled by default)
  4. Update cards proactively — when card expires in 30 days, reach out

Decline Tracking

Keep a simple log:

DateClientReasonOutcome
Review monthly to identify patterns.

Scripts

Initial Call

"Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] from Slate Systems. How's it going? I wanted to check in and make sure everything's running smoothly for you."

If They Ask About Payment

"Yeah, I did notice there might've been a small hiccup with the payment. Did you happen to get a new card recently? No big deal either way, just want to make sure we get it sorted."

Asking for New Card

"Would you mind giving me the new card number? I can update it right here and we'll be all set."

If No Answer (Voicemail)

"Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] from Slate Systems. Just checking in to make sure everything's going well. Give me a call back when you get a chance — [your number]. Talk soon!"

Text Follow-Up (If No Call Back)

"Hey [Name]! Just checking in. Is everything okay? Noticed a small issue with the account and wanted to make sure you're all set. Let me know!"

Escalation

If after 7 days:

  • 3 call attempts

  • 2 text attempts

  • No response


→ Send final email explaining service will be paused
→ Pause automations (don't delete account)
→ Try one more time in 2 weeks before canceling
