Escalation Rules

Role: Support / VA
Purpose: Know when to handle vs when to escalate
Last Updated: Feb 22, 2026

Core Principle

If in doubt, escalate. It's better to escalate unnecessarily than to mishandle a client issue.

Escalation Process

Step 1: Acknowledge the Client

Don't leave them hanging. Even if escalating, respond:

"I want to make sure this is handled right. I'm getting [Dan/appropriate person] involved and they'll reach out to you today."

Step 2: Notify the Right Person

For Fulfillment issues:
  • Tag in Slack/communication tool
  • Include: client name, issue, urgency
For Closer/Founder issues:
  • Call or text immediately
  • Don't wait for them to see a message

Step 3: Document

In the client's contact record:

  • Add note describing the issue

  • What you told the client

  • Who you escalated to


Step 4: Follow Up

If the escalation person hasn't responded in 2 hours:

  • Ping them again

  • Check in with client: "Still working on this for you"

What NOT to Do

‚ùå Never say:

  • "That's not my department"

  • "I don't know, figure it out"

  • "There's nothing I can do"


‚ùå Never do:
  • Process cancellations without conversation

  • Offer refunds or discounts

  • Make promises about timelines

  • Ignore messages


Scripts

Escalating to Founder/Closer

"I understand. Let me have [Dan] look into this personally. He'll reach out to you today to make sure everything's handled right."

Escalating to Fulfillment

"I see what's happening. I'm going to have our technical team take a look at this. They'll have it sorted out shortly. I'll follow up once it's fixed."

When you don't know

"Great question. Let me find out the exact answer and get back to you. Give me a few hours."
